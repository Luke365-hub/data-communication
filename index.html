02 — Data Communication

What Is Data Communication?

Data communication is the exchange of digital information between two or more devices through a transmission medium. It forms the backbone of the modern digital world — from a simple text message to complex cloud computing systems.

📡

Components

A data communication system has five key components: Sender, Receiver, Message, Transmission Medium, and Protocol. Each plays a vital role in ensuring data is sent and received accurately.

🔁

Transmission Modes

Data can be transmitted in three modes: Simplex (one direction only), Half-Duplex (both directions but not simultaneously), and Full-Duplex (both directions at the same time).

🌐

Network Types

Networks are classified by size and scope: LAN (Local Area Network), MAN (Metropolitan Area Network), and WAN (Wide Area Network). The internet is the largest WAN in existence.

📶

Signals

Data is carried by analog signals (continuous waves) or digital signals (discrete binary values). Bandwidth, frequency, and noise are critical factors that affect signal quality and data speed.

🔒

Protocols

Protocols are the rules governing data exchange. Key protocols include TCP/IP, HTTP/HTTPS, FTP, and SMTP. The OSI model provides a 7-layer framework for understanding these protocols.

⚡

Transmission Media

Data travels through guided media (twisted pair, coaxial cable, fiber optics) or unguided media (radio waves, microwaves, infrared). Fiber optics offers the highest speed and least interference.

The OSI Model – 7 Layers

7 Application Layer – User interface (HTTP, FTP, SMTP)
6 Presentation Layer – Data formatting & encryption (SSL)
5 Session Layer – Session management
4 Transport Layer – Reliable delivery (TCP, UDP)
3 Network Layer – Routing & IP addressing
2 Data Link Layer – Node-to-node delivery (MAC)
1 Physical Layer – Bits over physical medium
03 — The Telegraph

The Telegraph: Pioneer of Data Communication

The electric telegraph, invented in the 1830s–1840s, was the first system to transmit information electronically over long distances. It laid the groundwork for all modern data communication systems.

📅 1837

Samuel Morse and Alfred Vail develop the electric telegraph and Morse Code

🌊 1858

First transatlantic telegraph cable laid, connecting North America and Europe

💡 Legacy

The telegraph introduced the concept of digital binary signaling — the foundation of all modern computing
